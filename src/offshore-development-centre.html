An Offshore Development centre (ODC) is a dedicated remote facility or team established by an organization in a different country or location to handle specific software development projects or provide ongoing development and support services. It serves as an extension of the client's in-house development team, providing a collaborative and cost-effective approach to software development.


An ODC comprises a dedicated team of software developers, designers, testers, project managers, and other professionals who work exclusively on the client's projects. This team is handpicked and tailored to meet the specific needs of the client's software development requirements.


ODCs enable seamless remote collaboration between the client's in-house team and the offshore team. Communication and project management tools, such as video conferencing, project management software, and messaging platforms, facilitate real-time collaboration, ensuring effective coordination and alignment of project goals.


ODCs consist of highly skilled professionals with expertise in various technologies, programming languages, and industry domains. This diverse talent pool allows clients to tap into specialized skills that may not be readily available in their local market, enabling them to tackle complex projects and leverage the latest industry trends.


ODCs provide the flexibility to scale the development team up or down based on project requirements. This scalability allows clients to adapt quickly to changing business needs, market demands, or project scope. Whether the need is for additional resources for a large-scale project or temporary support for a specific phase, an ODC can adjust to the evolving needs.
